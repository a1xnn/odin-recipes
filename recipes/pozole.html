Pozole

Mexico's Tradional Soup

A staple Mexian soup commonly served at big events. There are many variations of pozole. Pozole rojo being the most common

Ingredients

  • Guajillo chiles
  • Canned white hominy
  • Pork shoulder cut into cubes
  • Oregano
  • cilantro
  • white onion
  • Red radishes
  • garlic

Instruction

  1. Boil 5 quarts of water inside a 10 quart stockpot
  2. Remove and discard the seeds and stems from the chiles. Heat a cast iron pan and heat the chilis until they soften.
  3. Bring a pot with 3 cups of water to a simmer and remove from heat. Add the chiles to the hot water and cover. Let the chiles soak for 20 minutes.
  4. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large saute pan on medium high heat. Add the pork pieces and brown the meat on all sides.
  5. Once the meat has browned, transfer it to the stockpot of boiling water. Aswell add the rinsed hominy, bay leaves, cumin and organo and tablespoons of salt. Bring to a simmer,reduce the heat and cook for 15 minutes.
  6. To prepare the red sauce, blend the chilies with 2 cups of water, a tablespoon of salt, and 4 cloves of garlic. Strain the red sauce through a sieve.
  7. Add a couple teaspoons of salt and lower the heat just enough to maintain a simmer,partially covered.
  8. Cook for 2 to 3 hours until the pork is completely tender.
  9. Slice the onions, cilantro and radishes for garnishes
  10. Once everything is ready serve and enjoy.
